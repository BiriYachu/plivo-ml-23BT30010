Why This Configuration Works

  • Sequence Compression: By training a BPE tokenizer specifically on the target train corpus, the model represents complex Devanagari (Hindi) characters in single tokens instead of 3 raw bytes, achieving a 2.19x compression. This doubles the amount of text visible to the attention layers in any block window.
  • Parameter Reinvestment: Enabling weight tying and replacing learned absolute positional embeddings with mathematical RoPE saved over 184K parameters. This allowed us to expand the model's width to 192 (from 160) without violating the 2M param cap, heavily improving representational capacity.
  • Optimal Optimization: Warmup prevents initial gradient explosion, cosine decay schedules stable local minima convergence, and AdamW weight decay prevents overfitting on the small corpus.
  • Context Length Scaling: Increasing the context window to 256 allows the attention mechanism to capture twice as much contextual context and doubles the data density trained per optimization step.
